StableDrop — How a payment works

A buyer's money is held safely until it's time to pay the seller — for goods or services (say, a builder finishing a job). Here's every step.

Off-chain (our app / database) On-chain (public blockchain)
Frontend (in your browser) Backend (our servers)
Non-custodial (we never hold the money)
1

Seller creates a payment request

Seller · in their browser

The seller — whether selling an item or providing a service (like a builder quoting for a job) — fills in an amount, a description, a release date, and the buyer's email. This just creates a record in our system — nothing happens with any money yet, and nothing is on the blockchain.

Off-chain Frontend Backend (saved)
2

Buyer gets a payment link

Buyer · by email

We email the buyer a link to the request (or the seller can share the link directly). Still no money and no blockchain involved — just a notification.

Off-chain Backend (email)
3

Buyer opens the link & signs in

Buyer · in their browser

The buyer opens the link and connects their own crypto wallet. They review the amount and the release date before agreeing to anything.

Off-chain Frontend
4

Buyer accepts → a secure escrow "vault" is created on the blockchain

Buyer accepts · vault created on-chain (we cover the blockchain fee)

When the buyer accepts, a brand-new escrow contract — think of it as a digital safe — is created on the public blockchain. It's programmed to only ever release money to the buyer or the seller. No money moves into it yet.

On-chain Non-custodial vault
5

Buyer approves & deposits the funds

Buyer · signs with their own wallet

The buyer approves the payment and the funds move directly from the buyer's wallet into the escrow vault. The buyer signs this themselves with their own wallet — their private key never leaves their device, and the money never touches a StableDrop wallet.

On-chain Signed in browser Non-custodial — we never hold it
6

Both parties are notified — money is now locked & protected

Buyer & Seller · by email

We email both sides to confirm the funds are safely locked in the vault until the release date. The buyer is protected; the seller knows the money is committed.

Off-chain Backend (email)
7

On the release date, the seller is paid automatically

Seller · paid directly on-chain

Once the release date passes (and there's no dispute), the funds go straight from the vault to the seller's wallet. Again, the money never passes through StableDrop — the blockchain enforces it.

On-chain Non-custodial payout
What if there's a problem?

Steps 1–7 are the smooth path. If the buyer isn't happy before the release date, they can open a dispute instead — and even then, StableDrop can never take the money.

A

Buyer opens a dispute

Buyer only · before the release date

If something's wrong (an item not received or not as described, or work that wasn't finished or done properly), the buyer can raise a dispute — but only while the money is still locked and only before the release date. Once that date passes with no dispute, the seller is simply paid as normal. The seller can't open a dispute; they just receive payment on time.

On-chain Buyer-initiated
B

The money freezes in the vault

Funds stay locked on-chain

The moment a dispute is opened, the funds are frozen inside the escrow vault. The seller can no longer auto-claim, and the money still doesn't move to anyone — it just waits until the dispute is settled. As always, it never goes to a StableDrop wallet.

On-chain Non-custodial — still locked in the vault
C

The split is decided by a 2-of-3 vote

Buyer · Seller · Arbiter (a neutral tie-breaker)

Each contract has three voters: the buyer, the seller, and a neutral arbiter who can only help decide — never take the funds. When any two of the three agree on how to split the money (e.g. full refund, full payment, or anything in between like 60/40), that decision is locked in.

On-chain 2-of-3 agreement
D

Funds are released — only ever to buyer and seller

Buyer and/or Seller · paid directly on-chain

The agreed split is paid out straight from the vault to the buyer and/or the seller. The blockchain physically prevents the money from going anywhere else — not to the arbiter, not to StableDrop. The two shares always add up to 100% of the escrow.

On-chain Non-custodial payout
Non-custodial, end to end — even in a dispute. StableDrop never holds your money. Funds go buyer → vault → seller (or back to the buyer if a dispute is resolved that way), all on the blockchain, controlled only by code. The arbiter and StableDrop can help decide a dispute, but the contract makes it impossible for them to take the funds. We just handle the easy parts — the request, the notifications, and covering the blockchain fees so you don't have to.
