StableDrop Liquid — Get your locked payout funded early

Got money locked in escrow that pays out later? StableDrop Liquid lets a funder advance you most of that cash today, at a small discount, so you don't have to wait for the payout date — without ever risking the money if the deal falls through.

The idea in one line: a seller is owed, say, $10,000 in 30 days (locked in escrow). They'd rather have ~$9,800 today. A funder advances them the cash now and, in return, becomes the person the escrow pays out to at maturity. The whole exchange happens atomically on the blockchain — money and payout rights change hands in the same instant, or not at all. Everything a funder needs to assess a cashflow is already public on the blockchain; our app just makes that data easy to browse.

1

The cashflow is available for funding

Automatic · seller can opt out

Any active escrow that pays out in the future can be found by funders by default. They see the amount, the payout date, and risk signals like dispute history. None of this is information StableDrop creates or owns — every escrow already lives on the public blockchain, where anyone can read it. Our app is just a convenient window onto that public data. Sellers who'd rather stay private can hide their escrow from our view at any time.

Off-chain (just a view) Data lives on-chain
2

A funder makes an offer

Funder · in their browser

A funder browses available cashflows, filters by payout date, amount, and discount, and makes an offer — "I'll advance you $9,800 now for your $10,000 payout." They set how long the offer stays open. Several funders can have offers on the same cashflow at once. No sign-up or KYC is needed to make an offer — any wallet with stablecoins can.

Off-chain Frontend Backend (offer)
3

The seller accepts one offer

Seller · in their browser

The seller compares the offers and accepts the one they like. The funding fee is shown up front, so the seller knows their exact take-home before agreeing. Accepting one offer automatically cancels all the others on that cashflow.

Off-chain Frontend
4

A one-time "swap vault" is created for this sale

On-chain · we cover the blockchain fee

A dedicated escrow-swap contract is created on the blockchain just for this deal. It's hard-wired with the exact terms — who the seller is, who the funder is, the agreed amount, the fee, and a payment deadline that's guaranteed to fall safely before the payout date. No money is in it yet.

On-chain Trustless — terms locked in code
5

The seller hands over the payout rights to the vault

Seller · signs with their own wallet

The seller points the escrow's payout at the swap vault. This is the only thing they hand over — and crucially, the vault can only ever do one of two things with it: give it to the funder (if they pay) or give it straight back to the seller (if they don't). The seller's money is never exposed to loss.

On-chain Signed in browser Seller stays protected
6

The funder pays — and everything swaps at once

Funder · paid directly, atomically

The funder sends the agreed payment. In a single, all-or-nothing transaction the vault checks there's no active dispute, then: the payout rights transfer to the funder, the seller receives their cash (minus the small fee), and StableDrop collects the fee. If any part can't complete — wrong amount, a dispute, or the deadline passed — the whole thing reverts and the funder is refunded. There's no in-between state.

On-chain Atomic swap — all or nothing
7

At maturity, the original escrow pays the funder

Funder · paid directly on-chain

The underlying escrow is untouched and works exactly as before — but now its payout goes to the funder, the new owner of the cashflow. The seller already got their cash in step 6. Everyone's done.

On-chain Non-custodial payout
What if the funder doesn't pay?

Steps 4–7 are the smooth path. If a funder accepts but never actually pays, the seller is fully protected — they simply take their cashflow back. Recovery and a completed deal can never both happen.

A

The payment deadline passes with no payment

Built-in safety timer

Every deal has a hard deadline for the funder to pay, and it's always set to expire safely before the escrow's real payout date. So the cashflow can never mature while a deal is in progress — there's always time for the seller to step in.

On-chain Deadline expired
B

The seller reclaims their cashflow

Seller only · signs with their own wallet

After the deadline, the seller calls "recover" and the payout rights snap straight back to them — no loss, no permission needed from anyone. They keep their full cashflow and can offer it for funding again later. The funder never paid, so nothing was owed to them.

On-chain Seller-only recovery No loss to the seller
Trustless and non-custodial, end to end. StableDrop Liquid never holds your money or your cashflow. The swap vault can only ever do two things — complete the deal (funder pays → seller gets cash, funder gets the payout) or hand the cashflow back to the seller after the deadline — and never both. No operator approval, no off-chain signature, no oracle, no one with special powers can interfere. StableDrop just provides the funding platform, takes a small fee at the moment of funding, and covers the blockchain fees.
